Ratatouille

Description

Ratatouille is a classic French Provençal stewed vegetable dish, featuring a colorful mix of eggplant, zucchini, bell peppers, tomatoes, and onions, seasoned with herbs like thyme and basil. It’s a hearty, wholesome meal that can be served as a main dish, side dish, or even a filling for crepes and omelets.

Ingredients

  • 1 large eggplant, diced
  • 2 zucchini, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, diced
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 4 tomatoes, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil leaves for garnish (optional)

Steps to Cooking

  1. Prepare the Vegetables: Dice the eggplant, zucchini, bell peppers, onion, and tomatoes into evenly sized pieces. Mince the garlic.
  2. Sauté the Vegetables: Heat the olive oil in a large skillet or pot over medium heat. Add the diced onion and garlic, and sauté until the onion is translucent.
  3. Add the Vegetables: Add the eggplant and cook for about 5 minutes until it starts to soften. Then, add the bell peppers and zucchini, and continue to cook for another 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  4. Cook the Tomatoes: Add the diced tomatoes to the skillet, along with dried thyme and basil. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Stir well to combine.
  5. Simmer the Ratatouille: Reduce the heat to low, cover the skillet, and let the vegetables simmer for about 30 minutes, stirring occasionally. The vegetables should be tender and well-cooked, with the flavors fully melded.
  6. Adjust Seasoning: Taste the ratatouille and adjust the seasoning if needed. Add more salt, pepper, or herbs according to your preference.
  7. Serve: Garnish with fresh basil leaves if desired and serve hot. Ratatouille can be enjoyed on its own, with crusty bread, over rice, or as a side dish.

Enjoy your ratatouille!
