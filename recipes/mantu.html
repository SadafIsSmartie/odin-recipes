Mantu

Description

Afghan Mantu is a popular traditional dish in Afghanistan, consisting of steamed dumplings filled with seasoned ground meat, usually beef or lamb, and onions. It is often topped with a savory sauce made from yogurt and split peas and garnished with dried mint, coriander, or fresh herbs. Afghan mantu is a cherished dish, typically prepared for special occasions, family gatherings, and celebrations.

Ingredients

For the Dough

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 3/4 cup water (adjust as needed)

    • For the Filling

  • 1 lb (450 g) ground beef or lamb
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste

    • For the Yogurt Sauce

  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt to taste

    • For the Split Pea Sauce

  • 1/2 cup yellow split peas, cooked until soft
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, diced (or 1/2 cup tomato sauce)
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • Salt and pepper to taste

    • Garnishes

  • Dried mint
  • Chopped fresh coriander (cilantro)
  • Ground red pepper (optional)

    • Steps

    1. Prepare the Dough: Mix the flour and salt in a bowl. Gradually add water and knead until you get a smooth, elastic dough. Cover and let it rest for 30 minutes.
    2. Make the Filling: In a bowl, combine the ground meat, chopped onion, garlic, coriander, cumin, salt, and pepper. Mix well.
    3. Prepare the Mantu Dumplings: Roll out the dough on a floured surface to about 1/8 inch thickness. Cut into 2-3 inch squares or circles.Place a small spoonful of filling in the center of each dough piece. Fold and pinch the edges to seal the dumpling.
    4. Steam the Mantu: Arrange the dumplings in a steamer, making sure they don’t touch. Steam for about 30-40 minutes until the dough is cooked through and the filling is done.
    5. Prepare the Yogurt Sauce:Mix the yogurt with minced garlic and salt. Set aside.
    6. Make the Split Pea Sauce: Sauté the onions until soft. Add the tomatoes or tomato sauce, cooked split peas, coriander, salt, and pepper. Simmer for about 10-15 minutes until thickened.
    7. Assemble and Serve: Arrange the steamed mantu on a serving dish. Spoon the yogurt sauce over them, followed by the split pea sauce. Garnish with dried mint, fresh coriander, and ground red pepper if desired.

    Enjoy your delicious Afghan Mantu!
